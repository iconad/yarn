Profile

A prominent investment company Emirates Strategic Investment Company LLC (ESIC) is mandated to achieve significant financial returns through amalgamating assets with a primary focus on real estate, equity investments and other specialized investments, positioning and integrating ESIC’s diversified portfolio within the global economy.

HISTORY

Over the past decade, the Group and its affiliates have engaged in diversified equity investments within prominent companies across various sectors. Some notable investments include First Abu Dhabi Bank (FAB) and Air Arabia. At our firm, we take a systematic approach to evaluating economic drivers and industry fundamentals in order to construct a sustainable, liquid, and long-term investment portfolio. This allows us to produce the best possible risk-adjusted returns by investing in companies that demonstrate sound management, strong financial performance, and strong corporate governance.

Additionally, the Group has redefined the real estate business, modernizing and advancing the industry by fostering a culture of partnerships in which all clients and listings are represented in an ethical and shared environment. Evolving the traditional model of how real estate is practiced, our forward-thinking property experts share their knowledge, areas of influence, contacts and expertise, ensuring clients a better representation and a true competitive edge.

Our approach has proven highly successful. Since its inception, the Group has grown an extensive diversified investment portfolio and property management portfolio constituting residential, commercial & retail properties. Additionally, the Group has vast residential & commercial sales experience and established itself as a prominent player in the real estate market, representing many of the country’s most visible & notable properties.

KEY FACTORS

Assets Under Management & Net Asset Value

Group currently operates in three strategic business segments:

  • Property Management: The Group owns and manages a portfolio of approximately over 3,500 units, a combination of residential and commercial units in the UAE. The Group tenant profiles include Government Departments, Foreign Diplomatic missions, Corporates and others. Most of the tenants have been with the Group for a long time as the Group maintains a strong relationship with its tenants by providing timely services and adequate property management.
  • Real Estate Investment (Residential & Commercial Development): this business segment is carried out to enhance the value of the existing undeveloped plots of land that the Group owns in Dubai and Abu Dhabi. It is principally undertaken through joint venture partnerships with internationally recognized property developers and is designed to deliver a return on the initial land invested in the project by the Group through the eventual sale of the properties and/or land developed. The Group also undertakes development projects intending to retain the developed property as a rental property to grow its portfolio of investment properties. Currently, the Group has an undeveloped land bank of approx. 1 million sq.ft. Major ongoing real-estate development projects for sale can be categorized as follows:
  • Joint Venture Arrangement
  • Six Senses The Palm, Dubai - The objective of the Six Senses project is to develop an ultra-luxury waterfront project on West Crescent of Palm Jumeirah, covering an area of 700,000 sq. Ft. The development will include 172 ultra-luxury residences, to date, approximately 70% of which have been sold. The residential development will be a branded residence that includes, 131 penthouses, a 60-key hotel along with the branded residence, 32 sky villas, and 9 signature beachfront villas. All housing units will be managed by Six Senses, which will provide residents with privileged access to the Six Senses Hotel’s top-class amenities in addition to the private beach, marina access, and much more. The construction of the project started in 2022, and the estimated date of completion is 2025.
  • Marina Gate Project - The objective was to develop three towers which are made-up of a mix of affordable and high-end luxury apartments, with state-of-the-art facilities in the Dubai Marina comprising of over 1,400 residential units. First two towers have been the real success story for the Group. First tower was completed and handed over in 2018 and second tower has been successfully completed and handed over in 2019. Third tower was also completed and handed over in 2020. Third tower included a branded residence with a tie-up with Jumeirah Hotels & Resorts, comprising of branded residential serviced apartment units specifically marketed to high-end customers. All the residential and retail inventory in the Project has been completely sold.
  • The Peninsula Project - The objective of this project is to develop a master waterfront development spanning 3.4 million sq. ft, comprising 6 premium residential towers, commercial facilities, and community amenities. Located in Business Bay's best location, the development offers panoramic views of the Dubai Canal on three sides, as well as easy access to Dubai's most sought-after locations. The sales of the Peninsula project launched in Q3 2021 and more than 2,200 units of the project have already been sold with an average price ranging from AED 1800 to AED 3900 per sq. ft. Investing in the Peninsula project is expected to yield significant returns with 100% of the sales achieved within weeks of the launch date, due to the detailed planning and high level of build quality.
  • Residential Projects Developed for Investment - The Group has recently completed the development of the following residential projects for long-term lease in the UAE:
  • Nasayem Building, In 2022, ESIC completed construction of a residential tower located in Abu Dhabi (Saadiyat Island) for long-term lease in the UAE with 8 three-bedroom Duplex , 40 two-bedroom, 29 one-bedroom and 38 Studios and nine commercial units.
  • Al Arabia Tower, a residential tower located in Abu Dhabi with 72 three-bedroom apartments and five commercial units, with a 99% occupancy rate for the residential units; and*
  • Lulu Tower, a residential tower located in Abu Dhabi with 72 two-bedroom apartments and five commercial units, with a 100% occupancy rate for the residential units. *
  • * Both residential towers were completed in Q3 2019 and based on strong demand for high-quality residential units, were fully occupied within the span of the first three months of 2020.
  • Labor camp, with 400 rooms located in Jabel Ali – Dubai.
    • There are several residential projects under construction for long-term lease in the UAE, which will be completed in the coming years.
  • Financial Investments: this business segment focuses on both public and private equity investments in order to diversify the Group’s revenue. The Group makes strategic investments in GCC companies across a number of different sectors, including the financial services, insurance, aviation and real estate development sectors. Currently, the Group’s financial investments include its shareholdings in First Abu Dhabi Bank PJSC (“FAB”), Abu Dhabi National Takaful Company PSC (“National Takaful”), Al Qudra Holding, Air Arabia PJSC (“Air Arabia”), Emaar Development PJSC (“Emaar”), and Dubai Islamic Bank PJSC.

Corporate Governance

Principals

ESIC has adopted a two-tiered governance structure consisting of an Executive Board and Committees. Governance is based on a strict separation of management functions fulfilled by the Executive Board and oversight functions performed by the Committees. ESIC governance relies on rigid, clearly-defined rules and absolute trust between the Executive Board and its Committees.

ESIC’s Corporate Governance Principles and Values are founded on:

  • Accountability
  • Responsibility
  • Transparency
  • Fairness
  • Trust

Executive Board of Directors

The Executive Board of Directors has an overriding and is mainly responsible for the company's absolute governance and liaison with its committees and shareholders.

The Board will monitor financial performance and external communication and collectively direct the company affairs whilst meeting the expectations of shareholders and stakeholders..

Further to business and financial aspects, the Board recognizes the importance of periodical board meetings for directors to discharge their responsibility to manage the company's strategy and policy and monitor delegated authorities to facilitate individual directors reporting on their particular areas of responsibility.

Executive Committee

The Executive Committee oversees all aspects of the ESIC's strategy, including diversification of investment sectors and asset classes, deployment, fundraising, and market environment analysis. The Executive Committee will also oversee the operational performance of investments, HR development, and our CSR policy.

Risk Committee

The Executive Board of Directors commissions the Risk Committee with an underlying task of assisting the Board in its oversight of maintaining a dynamic approach to risk management; having particular emphasis on:

  • Evaluating & Recommending ESIC's Risk Exposure & Tolerance
  • Risk Profile
  • Integrating Risk Policies & Procedures
  • Risk Governance Structure
  • Risk Operational Deficiencies
  • Investment & Financial Risk Limits
  • Effectiveness of ESIC’s Risk Management Framework in Supporting the Group

The Committee's extensive skills and experience contribute to considering how ESIC manages Group risk. Each member shall be entitled to rely on the consistency, accuracy & totality of information provided to the Committee.

Investment Committee

The Investment Committee is responsible for managing the existing investment portfolio, vetting new investment opportunities and contributing to business and investment planning.

The Investment Committee are tasked with the following responsibilities:

  • Investment team oversight
  • Establish guiding principles for investment activities
  • Endorse investment strategy
  • Endorse new investment opportunities
  • Monitor & manage investment portfolio
  • Endorse divestiture strategies of existing investments
  • Endorse financing instruments
  • Measure and prepare portfolio reviews, divestiture reviews and forthcoming investment strategies for the purpose of delivering reports and budgets

Audit & Compliance Committee

ESIC's audit & compliance committee is an essential pillar of effective corporate governance and is the sound body to offer effective oversight of the company's performance, independence and objectivity.

The audit committee is responsible for ESIC's financial reporting processes, internal controls, financial statements and auditor quality audits, and ESIC's compliance with legal & regulatory requirements. The audit committee is independent of safeguarding investors.

ESIC has a genuine view of its corporate responsibilities (“CSR”) by committing to advance the policies and systems across the Group, addressing all aspects of CSR that are relevant to the business.

These include ethical conduct, employee wellbeing, health and safety, sustainability of the environment and community involvement. We appreciate that our social, environmental and ethical conduct impacts our reputation, which is why ESIC is committed to continual improvement.

As part of our active investment approach, we regularly engage with the senior management of our portfolio companies to ensure they have a deep understanding of our core values of CSR.

The Board is ultimately accountable for CSR and is committed to implementing and enhancing appropriate policies while remaining steadfast to a fundamental commitment to sustaining long-term value for shareholders and all stakeholders.

ESG Strategy

ESIC will continue to strengthen its financial position through implementing consistent investments within the framework and criteria of ESG, thus generating competitive returns for its stakeholders.

ESIC approach towards its investment portfolio, its suppliers, employees, local communities, the environment, and society is an essential part of its success.

We believe that responsible investment and business success go hand in hand, as such, ESIC has embedded consideration of ESG factors throughout all stages of its different investment activities and asset classes both real and financial. We are fundamentally committed to build long-term, sustainable businesses, which will grow, provide employment, and generate economic benefits in an environmentally and socially responsible manner.

ESIC reliable investment is based on strong analysis, wise judgement of highly experienced staff, and excellent risk mitigation. ESIC will continue to invest in iconic high-quality asset classes to establish well diversified investment portfolio with reliable approach towards the environment, society, and corporate governance.

Before acquiring a business, ESIC investment team identifies ESG- related key risks or opportunities. ESIC ESG considerations may be company-specific or common to the industry/subsector or geography as a whole and include political and regulatory developments.

The considerations are researched through a variety of sources, including regulatory/compliance filings, investor disclosures and/or media reports. Within UAE law we also assess anti-bribery and corruption risk and seeks assurance that the company is fully compliant with the ESG laws and regulations in the relevant jurisdictions. This initial ESG assessment is an integral part of ESIC due diligence and overall risk and value creation assessment of the business.
